Career Options

Why Not You?

No matter whether you stayed at home with the children or worked outside the home, you have a wealth of experience and knowledge that few young people can imagine. Your point-of-view is representative of a certain generation that just happens to have the most expendable income and just happens to be the biggest market in the next several years.

Companies are looking for new applications to meet the growing demands of seniors; they are looking for workers who understand the older generation, and they are looking for people such as you who have an unparalleled work ethic.

Why shouldn't you be the next Grace Hopper, leading the industry in developing applications, websites, or the next big thing? Sure, you can't start at the top of the tech heap, but if you don't start now, when will you start?

How to Start

First, you need to honestly ask yourself what you want to be when you grow up. We are not our parents, retiring to a golf community at the age of 65. We can expect to live longer and contribute more for longer than any prior generation. Get involved now in order to make your biggest contribution!

Do you want to build websites? Program computers? Test for usability? Help businesses (including nonprofits) optimize their websites? There are so many career choices you can make! Check out what's available and of interest to you, and then talk with others already in the field to make sure it's what you want.

After you have chosen areas of interest, check out the educational requirements. These days, you don't necessarily need a computer science degree, and most training can be obtained from online programs that allow you to learn at your own pace.

Why Primetime?

This website and community are for you. We want to encourage other women around our age to explore career and educational opportunities. What kinds of jobs are out there?

To answer that question, let's ask a few more:

  • What about tech gets you excited?
  • Can you type quickly and accurately?
  • Are you interested in working from home or from an office?
  • Do you like solving problems?

You'll need to take some time to consider these and other questions before deciding where to look for jobs and/or training for those jobs, but here are some fields to consider:

  • web development
  • web design
  • product support
  • game or app development
  • game or app design
  • new fields added all the time!

Aren't I too old to work in tech?

Probably not. As you see from above, women of all ages are working in tech. And more older women are needed to provide the maturity, life experience, and specialized knowledge of the fastest-growing market around.

Assuming you can still communicate with others and are willing to put in the hours needed to create a skillset necessary to do the work, you can never be too old!

What's it like for a woman in tech?

That depends on a lot of things. We won't sugarcoat this. Tech is getting a lot of bad press, and for a reason. The tech world hasn't been kind to women. But then, when we first entered the business world, it wasn't kind to us, either. We were often relegated to support roles no matter how knowledgeable we were.

This is where we feel our age is an asset to be utilized. Women our age have experience with harassment, prejudice, and intolerance. We know our worth, and we aren't going to stand by while someone says it's less than it is.

By joining together to support each others, we can make an impact that the younger generation of women can't; and we can end up working with that younger generation to make tech better for all ages and all types of workers.
